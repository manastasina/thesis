\section*{Summary}
\phantomsection % allows correct anchoring of this section in toc
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Summary} %add to toc

Viruses infect all domains of life and establish complex interactions with their hosts to warrant their own replication by subverting and hijacking multiple cellular processes. Understanding virus-host interactions is critical for controlling spread of pathogenic viruses, developing vaccines and searching for antivirals. Besides that, understanding virus-host interactions allows deciphering complex cellular processes and provides useful tools for biotechnology.

My research is dedicated to influenza A virus, an important pathogen that infects humans worldwide, represents a constant health care threat and requires thorough control. Influenza A expresses a non-structural protein NS1 that is a key regulator of viral interactions with the host cell and an important virulence factor. Versatile functions of NS1 modulate multiple cellular functions to secure viral replication.

This work addresses several aspects of NS1-mediated modulation of core cellular processes. It describes the mechanism by which NS1 controls cellular transcription, studies modulation protein synthesis by NS1, and investigates the effects of NS1 protein length polymorphisms on development of antiviral responses. The presented results increase our understanding of influenza A virus-host interactions and can be further utilized in the search for antivirals and vaccine development. In addition, this work provides a biotechnological application of influenza A NS1 protein for improvement of cell-free translation system.
